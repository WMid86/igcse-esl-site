Interview Idioms Fixed Expressions 9 idioms
to hit the ground running
立即投入工作；迅速上手
To start a new job quickly and energetically, without needing much time to settle in.
"I'm confident I can hit the ground running from day one."
to think outside the box
跳出固有思维；创新思考
To approach problems in creative or unconventional ways.
"I was asked to think outside the box to find a solution for the client."
to go the extra mile
付出额外努力；做得更多
To put in more effort than is strictly required.
"I always go the extra mile to ensure clients are fully satisfied."
to be a team player
善于团队合作
To work cooperatively and contribute to a shared goal.
"I consider myself a strong team player who supports colleagues when needed."
to wear many hats
身兼数职；一人多能
To take on multiple roles or responsibilities.
"In my last role, I wore many hats — from project manager to client liaison."
to bring something to the table
贡献某种价值或技能
To offer a specific skill, idea, or value to a team or organisation.
"I believe I bring strong analytical skills to the table."
to learn the ropes
熟悉工作；学习基本知识
To learn the basics of a new job or situation.
"It took a few weeks to learn the ropes, but I quickly became independent."
to be on the same page
达成共识；看法一致
To have a shared understanding or agreement with others.
"I always make sure the whole team is on the same page before starting a project."
to rise to the challenge
迎难而上；应对挑战
To successfully deal with a difficult situation or demanding task.
"When the team leader left unexpectedly, I rose to the challenge and stepped up."
Study tip: For the best results in an interview, practise using these words and idioms naturally in full sentences — not just memorising their meanings. Try recording yourself answering common interview questions using at least 3–5 words or idioms from each section.
