Reading Strategies for Paper 1 (0511)

This guide offers strategies for each reading exercise in Paper 1 of the Cambridge IGCSE ESL exam. Use these tips to improve your comprehension and perform confidently on exam day.

Exercise 1 – Skimming and Scanning

  • Skim first: quickly read the title, headings and first lines of paragraphs to get the gist of the passage. Note any obvious topic words.
  • Scan for keywords: before answering each question, underline important words in the question. Then scan the passage for those keywords or synonyms to locate the answer.
  • Read around the answer: once you find the relevant section, read the sentence before and after to understand the context.
  • Keep answers concise: use short phrases or a couple of words as instructed. Avoid copying large chunks of text.

Exercise 2 – Multiple Matching

  • Read all the options first: familiarise yourself with the list of statements or headings before reading the text.
  • Identify main ideas: each paragraph usually focuses on a single idea. Match it with the statement that best summarizes it.
  • Underline evidence: mark words or phrases that support your chosen match. This helps if you need to reconsider.
  • Eliminate wrong choices: cross out options once used to narrow down the remaining possibilities.

Exercise 3 – Note‑Taking

  • Read the headings: the note‑taking task provides headings and subheadings. Quickly scan them so you know what to listen for.
  • Use bullet points: write notes as brief points or phrases. Full sentences are unnecessary.
  • Paraphrase: use your own words. You don’t have to change every word, but avoid copying whole sentences.
  • Stick to word limits: if the question specifies a maximum number of words per note, do not exceed it.
